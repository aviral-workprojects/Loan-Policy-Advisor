SBI (State Bank of India) Personal Loan — Xpress Credit and Products (2026)

Sources: SBI Bank Direct, ClearTax, Paisabazaar (Mar 2026)
Data confidence: High | Last updated: March 2026
Largest public sector bank in India — widest reach, lowest income threshold

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Age: 21 to 76 years at loan maturity (updated; max repayment age 76 for personal loan)
Minimum monthly income: ₹15,000 standard; ₹5,000 for government/defence/pensioners under Xpress Credit
CIBIL score: 750 or above (strict_cibil_cutoff = Yes per 2026 data; previously reported as 700)
Employment: Government/PSU employees (primary target), defence, para-military, coast guards, pensioners; also private salaried and self-employed
Work experience: Minimum 12 months
Salary account: MANDATORY for Xpress Credit — salary/pension must be routed through SBI

CRITICAL NOTE ON CIBIL: SBI strict_cibil_cutoff is YES per 2026 research.
The 700 floor in prior data was from older sources. Current policy requires 750.
Scores 700–749 may qualify with enhanced documentation at higher rates.

XPRESS CREDIT REQUIREMENT:
Salary account with SBI is a hard prerequisite for standard Xpress Credit processing.
Applicants without SBI salary account must apply through an alternative route with additional documentation.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
INTEREST RATES (2026)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Starting rate: 10.00% per annum
Range: 10.00% to 15.1% p.a.
Government employees: Starting 10.45% (special rate)
Defence/Para-military: Special rate 10.45% (Shaurya variant)
Private sector employees: 12% to 14%
Self-employed: 13% to 15%
SBI uses 2-year MCLR (currently 8.75%) as pricing floor — slower-moving than private banks
Processing fee: Up to 1.50% (min ₹1,000, max ₹15,000); NIL for existing SBI customers

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
LOAN FEATURES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Loan amount: Up to ₹35 lakh (Xpress Credit standard); up to ₹20 lakh was older limit
Tenure: 6 to 84 months (updated; 72 months was prior data; 84 months available for select products)
Processing fee: Up to 1.50% (min ₹1,000, max ₹15,000)
No processing fee for existing SBI customers (major advantage)
No prepayment penalty after 6 months (better than HDFC's 12-month lock-in)
Interest calculated on daily reducing balance (regulatory compliance)

XPRESS ELITE:
For net monthly income above ₹1 lakh
Higher loan amounts available above standard ₹35 lakh cap
Rate starting 11.05% (but updated data shows 10.45% for government, 10.00% base)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SBI PRODUCT VARIANTS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Xpress Credit: Standard personal loan for salaried with SBI salary account
Xpress Elite: High-income earners (>₹1 lakh/month)
Pension Loan: For SBI pensioners with TPG (Third Party Guarantee)
Defence/Shaurya: Specialized for defence and para-military (rate from 10.45%)

HOME LOAN CONTEXT (relevant for cross-product advisory):
SBI is the largest mortgage provider in India
Starting rate: 7.25% (lowest among all major lenders as of March 2026)
Max tenure: 30 years
Female borrowers: 0.05% rate concession when listed as primary applicant
LTV: Up to 90% financing

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
DOCUMENTS REQUIRED
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Mandatory: PAN Card, Aadhaar Card
Identity: Passport, Voter ID (any one)
Income: Salary certificate or Form 16; last 3 months salary slips
Banking: Last 6 months bank statements (SBI account preferred)
Employment: Appointment letter or employer verification letter
Pensioners: Pension Payment Order (PPO), life certificate

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ADVANTAGES OVER PRIVATE BANKS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Lowest income threshold: ₹5,000 for government/defence — no private bank matches this
Widest reach: Largest branch network in India — accessible in tier 3/4 cities
Zero processing fee for existing customers: Saves ₹3,000–₹15,000 vs private banks
Pension loan availability: Covers post-retirement borrowing (unique to SBI)
Government employee preference: Best rates and easiest processing for central/state government
PMAY integration: SBI provides credit-linked subsidy scheme (CLSS) under PMAY for eligible applicants

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
DISADVANTAGES AND WARNINGS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Speed disadvantage: Public bank processing typically 5–7 days vs 24–48 hours for Axis/ICICI
Salary account mandatory: Xpress Credit not available without SBI salary account
CIBIL threshold raised: 750 now (not 700 as per older data)
Limited digital journey: Less advanced online/app process compared to ICICI/Axis
Private sector applicants pay higher rates: 12%–14% vs 10%–11.05% for government

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
REJECTION TRIGGERS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

No SBI salary account: Cannot use Xpress Credit product (most common rejection cause)
CIBIL below 750: High rejection probability for standard products
Income below ₹15,000 (non-government): Rejection for standard products
Self-employed with less than 12 months: Rejection

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
RECOMMENDATION LOGIC
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

For low-income government employees: SBI Xpress Credit is the ONLY major bank serving ₹5,000 income
For defence/para-military: Dedicated Shaurya product with lowest rates (10.45%)
For no-fee processing: Zero processing fee for existing SBI customers is unmatched
For pension borrowers: Only SBI among major banks has a dedicated pension loan product
For home loans: SBI offers lowest home loan rate (7.25%) with 30-year tenure
Female applicants (home loan): List wife as primary applicant to get 0.05% rate concession
For early repayment: No penalty after 6 months (better than HDFC's 12-month lock-in)
