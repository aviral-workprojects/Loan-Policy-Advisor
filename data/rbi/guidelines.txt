RBI Guidelines on Personal Loans and Consumer Credit (2024)

REGULATORY FRAMEWORK:
The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) regulates personal lending through the Banking Regulation Act, 1949 and various Master Circulars.

KEY REGULATIONS FOR PERSONAL LOANS:

1. FAIR PRACTICES CODE:
- Banks must disclose interest rate, fees, and all charges upfront
- No hidden charges permitted
- Standardized loan agreement required
- Cooling-off period: 3 days after loan sanction to cancel without penalty

2. INTEREST RATE REGULATIONS:
- Banks must use transparent benchmark rates (MCLR/External Benchmark)
- Internal rate of return must be disclosed
- Interest on daily reducing balance basis
- No pre-payment penalty on floating rate loans

3. KYC COMPLIANCE:
- Mandatory for all borrowers
- Aadhaar-based e-KYC permitted
- Video KYC for digital loans

4. CREDIT BUREAU REQUIREMENTS:
- Banks must report all loans to credit bureaus (CIBIL, Equifax, Experian, CRIF)
- All lenders must check CIBIL before disbursing
- Credit history must be checked for fraud prevention

5. DIGITAL LENDING GUIDELINES (2022):
- Loan disbursement only to borrower's bank account
- No automatic credit limit increase without consent
- Clear disclosure of APR (Annual Percentage Rate)
- Data privacy compliance mandatory

6. RECOVERY PRACTICES:
- No harassment permitted in loan recovery
- Recovery agents must have ID cards
- Calling hours: 8 AM to 7 PM only
- Borrowers can approach Banking Ombudsman for grievances

7. CONSUMER PROTECTION:
- Right to be heard — banks must acknowledge complaints within 5 days
- Banking Ombudsman scheme for unresolved complaints
- Right to know all charges before loan disbursement

8. CIBIL SCORE GUIDELINES:
- Banks are recommended to check credit reports from all 4 bureaus
- 300-900 scale
- RBI does not set minimum CIBIL requirements — each bank sets own policy

COMPLIANCE GUARDRAILS FOR LOAN ADVISORS:
- Must not guarantee loan approval
- Must disclose all applicable rates and charges
- Must not misrepresent bank terms
- Must advise customers to read loan documents carefully
