CREDIT SCORE TIERS AND BANK BEHAVIOR
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CIBIL 750 and above:
Best rates (9.98%–12%), pre-approved offers, instant disbursal at ICICI/Axis
All four major banks will approve; negotiate on spread

CIBIL 700–749:
Approved at standard rates (12%–16%)
ICICI most accommodating (700 strict floor, approval difficulty = Low)
Axis: Will consider but at higher rate band
HDFC: Approved but rate band 13%–18%
SBI: Increasingly strict; 750 is preferred cutoff

CIBIL 650–699:
Rejection likely from Axis and SBI (750 strict cutoff)
HDFC: Possible with enhanced documentation (600 is cited floor)
ICICI: Rejection below 700
Alternative: Bajaj Finserv (685 floor), NBFCs (no minimum but rates 18%–36%)

CIBIL below 650:
Systematic rejection across all Tier-1 banks
Redirect to: Fintechs (KreditBee, MoneyTap: rates 24%–36%)
Best path: Step-UP credit card (SBM Bank co-branded) to build credit history
Estimated credit-building timeline: 6–12 months of clean credit card usage

NEW TO CREDIT (NTC) / NO CIBIL:
HDFC and ICICI use alternate data (bank statements, employment verification)
SBI and Axis rely heavily on CIBIL history — difficult path for NTC
Recommendation: HDFC digital journey for NTC borrowers
Prerequisite: SBM Bank Step-UP card to build CIBIL before applying to public banks

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
EMI-TO-INCOME RATIO BENCHMARKS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

40% DTI: Comfortable approval zone — all major banks approve
40%–50% DTI: Borderline — ICICI most flexible, HDFC and Axis strictly enforce 50%
50%–65% DTI: ICICI may approve; others will reject
Above 65%: Systemic rejection — debt burden too high regardless of income

DTI Formula: (Existing EMIs + Proposed EMI) / Net Monthly Income

EMI Reference Table:
₹5 lakh at 12% for 36 months: EMI = ₹16,607
₹10 lakh at 11% for 48 months: EMI = ₹25,850
₹10 lakh at 10% for 60 months: EMI = ₹21,247
₹20 lakh at 10% for 72 months: EMI = ₹32,258
Golden rule: Proposed EMI should not exceed 40% of net monthly salary

Income-to-loan multiplier: Banks typically lend 10–20x monthly income
₹20,000/month income: Eligible for ₹2 lakh–₹4 lakh loan
₹50,000/month income: Eligible for ₹5 lakh–₹10 lakh loan
₹1 lakh/month income: Eligible for ₹10 lakh–₹20 lakh loan

EMPLOYER CATEGORY IMPACT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Category A employers (top MNCs, NIFTY 500, PSUs): Best rates, fastest approvals at all banks
Category B employers (established private companies): Standard rates
Category C employers (SMEs, lesser-known companies): Higher rates; possible additional documentation
Gig economy/freelancers: Bank statement analysis as proxy for income — ICICI most accommodating

Employment sector priority (best to worst for loan approval):
1. Government/Defence/PSU (best — SBI specialty)
2. Category A MNCs (best at private banks)
3. Reputed private companies
4. SME employees
5. Self-employed professionals (doctors, CAs — verified income)
6. Self-employed non-professionals (business owners)
7. Gig economy/freelancers (most difficult — bank statement analysis required)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
MARKET HEURISTICS FROM PAISABAZAAR DATA
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Multiple applications harm CIBIL: Each hard inquiry drops score by 5–10 points
Best strategy: Apply to one bank at a time; use pre-eligibility checkers (soft inquiry) first
Salary account advantage: Target bank salary account triggers pre-approved status worth 1%–2% rate reduction
Co-applicant benefit: Adding a co-applicant with good CIBIL can boost approval probability by 40%
Debt clearance: Paying off one existing EMI before applying improves DTI and can shift CIBIL tier
Avoid job changes: Do not change employers within 6 months before loan application

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FINTECH vs TRADITIONAL BANK POSITIONING
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Traditional banks (Axis, HDFC, ICICI, SBI): Rates 9.98%–16%; strict criteria; lower risk
NBFCs (Bajaj Finserv, Fullerton): Rates 11%–24%; medium criteria; moderate risk
Fintechs (KreditBee, MoneyTap, Navi): Rates 24%–36%; lenient criteria; high cost
Last resort: Fintechs are appropriate ONLY when traditional banks have rejected and credit-building is not immediately possible

Advisory guardrail: Never recommend fintech loans without disclosing the effective cost.
A ₹5 lakh loan at 36% for 24 months costs ₹2 lakh more in interest than the same loan at 12%.
